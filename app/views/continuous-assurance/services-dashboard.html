The “Secure Posture Continuous Assurance: Digital Services Dashboard” within Splunk is designed to support the Department for Education (DfE) Digital Services in demonstrating compliance with the Secure by Design (SbD) initiative. This initiative mandates that digital services include resources for building robust security practices. The dashboard provides continuous assurance by offering insights into outstanding security vulnerabilities and facilitating the planning of remediation efforts.

How can the dashboard help your service?

The Digital Services dashboard is crucial for ensuring that DfE’s digital services adhere to security policies and processes, thereby hardening their security posture. By leveraging data from various sources, the dashboard helps digital services demonstrate compliance with SbD, ensuring that critical systems and digital assets are secure. This not only enhances the security of the services but also builds confidence in the underlying technology platforms.

Key feature and capabilities

Continuous assurance

  • provides daily insights into outstanding security vulnerabilities for each digital service
  • facilitates the planning and remediation of security issues

Posture hardening

  • ingests data from Microsoft Defender for Cloud, CIS Benchmarks, GitHub and Qualys to test and improve security configurations
  • offers alerts on known vulnerabilities and security issues

Asset attribution

  • maintains a database of all Azure assets and GitHub repositories, attributed to specific digital services
  • uses a taxonomy based on Financial Business Partners (FBP) to categorise assets

Compliance measurement

  • implements controls or use cases in Splunk to measure compliance with specific policies
  • outputs compliance status as either 'Compliant' or 'Non-Compliant'

Target audience

The primary users of the Splunk dashboard are:

Digital service teams
Responsible for managing the architecture and configuration of their applications
Security and compliance teams
Ensuring that digital services adhere to security security policies and processes.
Service owners and contacts
Overseeing the security posture of their respective digital services
